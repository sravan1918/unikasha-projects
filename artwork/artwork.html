ARTWORKS

Painting:

The creation of images using pigments on surfaces such as
canvas, paper, or wood. This includes oil paintings, watercolors, acrylics, and more.

Sculpture:

Three-dimensional artworks created by shaping or carving
materials such as stone, metal, wood, clay, or glass. Sculptures can be freestanding or relief-based.

Drawing:

The creation of images using lines, tones, and textures.
Drawing can be done with various tools such as pencils, pens, charcoal, or pastels on paper or other surfaces.

Photography:

The art of capturing and creating images using a camera.
Photographers use composition, lighting, and other techniques
to convey meaning or aesthetics.

Printmaking:

The creation of artworks by transferring ink or pigment from a
matrix (such as a plate or block) onto paper or other materials.
Printmaking techniques include etching, engraving, lithography,
screen printing, and more.

Mixed Media:

Artworks that incorporate multiple materials or techniques, combining
elements such as painting, collage, assemblage, and digital media.

Digital Art:

Art created using digital tools and technologies, often involving
computer graphics, digital painting, or manipulation of images and visuals.

Performance Art:

Live artistic presentations or actions that involve the artist's body,
gestures, movement, or interaction with the audience. Performance art can
encompass theater, dance, music, spoken word, and other forms of live performance.
